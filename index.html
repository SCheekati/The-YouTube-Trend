Introduction

YouTube is the world's largest video sharing platform available for creators and businesses to host their content. The trending page is notable for boosting viewership and putting videos in the spotlight for people to like, share, comment, and subscribe to the respective channels.

Trending YouTube Video Statistics Kaggle dataset provides months of top trending videos from 10 countries. Up to 200 trending videos per day are provided in the dataset. Features include video title, video id, channel title, publish time, tags, views, likes, dislikes, description, comment count, trending date, thumbnail link, comments disabled, ratings disabled, and video error or removal.

Problem Statement

Content creators on YouTube work to create a trending video in hopes of increasing exposure and earning revenue. YouTube claims that viral videos are based on view count, how fast the views are coming from, where they are coming from etc., but the inner workings of the YouTube Algorithm are known to be unclear and ambiguous.

In this project, we’ll use a one-class classification (OCC) approach to predict possible trending videos on YouTube. We’ll use trending videos’ statistics as features to train five different machine learning models, compare each model and create an ensemble method overall.

Methods

Dataset preparation: Trending YouTube Video Statistics Kaggle dataset will be our main dataset for the training phase. On the other hand, we’ll also collect non-trending videos in the same timeframe for testing purposes. This step also includes dataset cleaning and potential feature pruning according to different ML models that we use.

Model training: Each student will implement and train at least one ML model on our task. We’ll study and compare each model in depth, and at the end create an ensemble model by merging all models. In summary, we will try
a. One-class SVM [1] or One-class random forests [2]
b. One-class CNN trained on video thumbnails [3]
c. Textual one-class NN on video descriptions/ title [4]
d. One-class autoencoder [5]
e. Supervised binary classification with SVM or NN.

Evaluation: A set of raw trending and non-trending video statistics will be used to evaluate the performance of each model. We’ll build a confusion matrix for each model and analyze the effectiveness of features.

Potential Results & Discussion

With our model, we will attempt to perform cross validation for all binary classifiers included. Binary classifiers with hyperparameters that adjust sensitivity can be judged by the area under their ROC curves. Otherwise, precision, recall and F1 score are well-defined and can be used for model evaluation.

We will compare the results of one-class classifiers against the results of traditional binary classifiers. The latter, as stated before, will have access to the negative-labeled data, which the former will not. Similar results would suggest that one-class classifiers are working as intended.
